                                  Justin Weiss Mod kamal namdeo • 4 years ago

                                  When you browse sites, the site can tell your browser to store small bits of information. That's really all a cookie is -- a small, specially formatted piece of data that's shared between your site and a specific user's browser.

                                  If you store data right in the cookie, there's nothing your server needs to do -- it just reads what it needs to read out of the cookie sent by the browser. (Rails has ways to make sure the cookie data hasn't been faked).

                                  Hope that helps!

                                          Justin Weiss Mod kamal namdeo • 3 years ago

                                          Sure! There's a good overview here: http://guides.rubyonrails.o...

                                          Rails can either leave the cookie insecure, it can sign the cookie with a secret known only to your app (that will detect tampering), and it can encrypt the cookie data entirely, so nothing but the app will be able to read it at all. As of Rails 4, encryption is the default.
